Colossians 3: 23-24

Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters, since you know that you will receive an inheritance from the Lord as a reward. It is the Lord Christ you are serving.

No matter what we do, no matter what our circumstances, only the Lord God can judge us according to our works. So whatever we do in life, if ever you are a student, employee, or whatever let us do it wholeheartedly to glorify our almighty Lord. Let us have one mind and one heart that we serve our Lord.

When we are in the human look we cannot attain bliss in what we do but when we have the Lord to look at, no matter how hard we do, it makes it easy to happen. Because living for the Lord, builds strong relationships with ourselves, our family, friends, and co-workers, schoolmates, and so on. Our Lord does not neglect what he sees in everything we do. God has a rewarding gift for each of us who serve Him and not for man.

This verse continues Paul's instructions to Christian "bondservants," or slaves. The concept he presents, however, is phrased in a very general way. The principle applies to all believers, for all those we work for: serve as if serving God Himself. According to the next verse, this is for a good reason. Our ultimate rewards are with God, not the human beings we serve for only a short time.
